New Regulations for Short-Term Rentals in South Africa: What You Need to Know

By Portia Buthelezi Policy Analyst, APASA

Key Changes in the 2024 Regulations

The South African government has introduced new regulations affecting short-term rental properties, set to take effect from March 2024. These changes will significantly impact how accommodation providers operate their businesses.

1. Registration Requirements

All short-term rental properties must now be registered with local municipalities. Key requirements include:

  • Proof of ownership
  • Safety compliance certificates
  • Insurance documentation
  • Tax clearance certificates

2. Safety Standards

Enhanced safety measures are now mandatory for all properties:

  • Fire safety equipment installation
  • Emergency evacuation plans
  • Regular safety inspections
"These new regulations aim to professionalize the short-term rental sector and ensure guest safety while maintaining property values in residential areas." - Department of Tourism

Impact on Accommodation Providers

The new regulations will require significant adjustments from property owners and managers. Here's what you need to do to ensure compliance:

Immediate Actions Required

  1. Review current property documentation
  2. Schedule safety inspections
  3. Update insurance policies
  4. Begin registration process

Important Deadlines

  • Registration Opens: January 30, 2024
  • Compliance Deadline: March 31, 2024
  • First Inspection Due: April 15, 2024

How APASA Can Help

  • Guidance on registration processes

  • Compliance workshops
